Terms & Definitions table

Term Definition Symbol Unit
Charge Current × Δtime, measured in coloumbs(not a base unit, that's amperes). Charge of a electron = −1.6x10−19C. Q C
Current Rate of flow of charge. Measured by an ammeter(connected in series) in amperes. 1amp = 1 coloumb per sec. Current flows from − to +, electron flow goes from + to −. I=ΔW⁄Q Note: Ammeter must have very small resistance. I A
Potential difference(PD) Measured in volts by voltmeter(connected in parallel). It is the energy transfered by unit charge that flows between two points.Energy transferred is the same as work done. 1volt = 1 joule per coloumb. Note: Voltmeter must have very large resistance. V V
Electrical energy (Energy transferred) The amount of energy transffered by a component. W/E J
Power Rate of energy transfer measured, in Watts. Many equations scroll down to see them.(J/S) P W
Restistance Ratio of the potential difference across the component compared to the current flowing throught it. R=V⁄I. Measured in ohms, calcuated using current and PD or measured using a Datalogger. When doing calcuations make sure the calculatons refer to the same component or system. R Ω
Resistivity 1) Resistivity of a material is the resistance across a metre lenth of wire. 2) Resistivity of a material is the resistane between two opposite faces of a 1m3 specimen of the material. ρ Ωm
Electromotive force (EMF) NOT A FORCE!EMF is energy gained per unit charge from the power supply. Measured in volts. ε−v1−v2−v3=0. When charge passes through a power supply some workis done by the power source, making charge gain eletric potential energy. ε V
Internal resistance The resistance inside a cell or battery, note the symbol is a small r not a big R. r Ω
Potential dividers Two or more resistors in series with each other, with a source of fixed PD. N/A N/A
